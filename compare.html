Everything else, that these apps don't even attempt

This isn't a checkbox comparison — for most of what's below, there's nothing on the other side to check. These are things no app on this page does at all, not features they do worse.

AI Diabetes Assistant

A chat & voice assistant that actually knows your data — your real readings, A1C, meds, and goals — not a generic chatbot. Ask it a question, or just tell it "log my metformin" or "I had 40 carbs for lunch" and it logs it for you.

Fasting Tracker + glucose correlation

Live countdown for 16:8, 18:6, 20:4, OMAD or a custom window, plus per-fast time-in-range and hypoglycemia stats — and a cross-fast insight comparing your glucose during fasting windows vs. outside them, once you've logged enough fasts to see a real pattern.

Meal history & pattern detection

Log a food once and GlucoBeacon remembers what it did to you — "last time you ate this, your reading was 210." Automatic pattern detection flags foods that consistently run you high, across 14 different pattern types.

Restaurant Advisor

53 real chains, 3,700+ menu items pre-scored by glycemic impact into Best Choices / Good Options / Higher Carb — plus AI nutrition estimates for any restaurant that isn't in the database yet. Your favorite local mom-and-pop spot isn't left out either: remember it once with your usual dishes, and it behaves just like any other chain from then on.

Pantry, recipes & shopping list

Scan groceries into a pantry that auto-deducts when you cook. A 120-recipe library with real Keto/Vegetarian filtering shows what you can make right now from what's already on hand.

Appointments, scheduled by voice

A real calendar with conflict warnings — and you can just say "schedule my endocrinologist for next Tuesday at 2" and it's on the calendar, no typing required.

Diet-type intelligence

Real Keto and Vegetarian badges computed from actual net-carb math, not a guess — plus one-tap AI suggestions to modify any dish ("ask for it lettuce-wrapped instead") to fit your diet.

Home screen widget

Your latest reading, steps, and calories at a glance — with a one-tap AI voice shortcut right from your home screen, no need to even open the app.

The science behind our numbers

Every calculation in GlucoBeacon traces back to a published clinical source — not a guess, and not a marketing number.

A1C Estimate

Nathan/ADA formula: (weighted average + 46.7) ÷ 28.7, weighted 1.0/0.5/0.25 for the most-recent/middle/oldest third of your last 90 days of readings — recent control counts more than a reading from 3 months ago. Requires at least 7 readings before an estimate is shown.

Time in Range & Glucose Variability

Built on the international consensus on continuous glucose monitoring metrics (Battelino et al., Diabetes Care 2019), adopted into the ADA Standards of Care: 70–180 mg/dL as target range, hypoglycemia split into Level 1 (<70) and clinically significant Level 2 (<54), and coefficient of variation for day-to-day stability — tightened to <33% when insulin or a sulfonylurea is logged, per ADA guidance on hypoglycemia risk for those drug classes.

Glycemic Index Data

Our 176-food curated database traces to real lab-tested GI values (ISO 26642 methodology: fasted subjects, a 50g-carb portion, blood glucose tracked over 2 hours against a glucose reference). For mixed dishes never tested as a whole — nobody has run a clinical trial on "chicken enchiladas" — we compute a carb-weighted estimate and label it as an estimate, since even that standard technique runs 22–50% off a true lab-measured result.

Daily Carb Budget

Mifflin-St Jeor equation for basal metabolic rate, adjusted by activity level and goal, then split by your chosen carb-approach percentage — the same free tool available on our Carb Calculator page.

Net Carbs

Standard convention: Total Carbs − Fiber − (Sugar Alcohols ÷ 2) — fiber isn't digested into glucose, and sugar alcohols are absorbed only partially.

Processed Food Score

A 1–10 score informed by the NOVA food classification system (Monteiro et al.), applied across every GI food, restaurant item, and barcode-scanned product — alongside carbs and glycemic index, since how processed a food is affects more than just its carb count.
